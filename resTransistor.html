Resonant Tunneling Transistors

Role

Tunneling is an electron transport mechanism that becomes dominant when device dimensions become comparable to the electron's wavelength, about 10 nm in semiconductors. Resonant-tunneling transistors offer the possibility of achieving increased function per unit area, and raise the prospects for progress beyond scaled integrated circuit technology. The nature and timing of the resonant-tunneling transistor opportunity would depend on developments in existing circuit technologies as well as advances in epitaxial materials growth, circuit design, and architectures. The development of a silicon resonant-tunneling device technology that is compatible with silicon processing technology could hasten the transfer of quantum devices into applications. In this event, the mature fabrication technology of Si could be combined with the high functional density of resonant-tunneling devices.
